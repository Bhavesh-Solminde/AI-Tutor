# We will be using StudyFetch and Gizmo AI for reference of our project

## Borrow from StudyFetch:

- The side-by-side layout — uploaded material on one side, tutor chat on the other. Clean and practical.
- Skill level selection (High School / College / Professional) at onboarding — feeds directly into your mastery rating system
- The "Tutor Me" adaptive pacing concept — only move forward when the student is ready

## Borrow from Gizmo:

- The gamified quiz flow with hearts/lives — makes quizzes feel low-stakes and fun rather than a test
- Spaced repetition logic for the Progress Tracking agent — surfaces weak topics automatically over time
- The "Explain" button after a wrong answer — directly maps to your "never repeat the same explanation" feature

## Where you beat both of them:
This is the most important part. Neither StudyFetch nor Gizmo does what you're building:

- Neither has a visual syllabus roadmap where topic nodes change color as mastery improves — that's entirely yours
- Neither has a comprehension checkpoint mid-explanation — both dump explanations and wait for you to ask. Your tutor actively pauses and checks in
- Neither has an exam countdown rescue plan — Gizmo has spaced repetition but no "my exam is in 3 days, build me a plan" flow
- Neither shows a mastery delta ("you went from 3/10 to 8/10") — both track progress but don't surface the before/after emotion


## So use them for UX inspiration on the file upload flow, quiz card design, and chat interface — but make sure your demo clearly shows the three things neither of them have. That's your competitive angle even against production apps with millions of users.
